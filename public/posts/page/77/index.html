Why is consciousness so hard to think about?

Derek asked whether I was agreeing with Roger Penrose by suggesting that we are missing some fundamental key to the understanding of consciousness and reality generally. I’m not arguing that the brain can only be simulated by a quantum computer. I’m only suggesting that the ‘deep’ problems always seem to be just over the horizon. There seems to be something essentially hard about distinguishing thoughts from the titanic amount of computations in the brain.
